\begin{abstract}
\noindent
We extend the disaggregated Keynesian cross of \citet{Teglio2025} with
endogenous investment and capital accumulation. Production is a
\emph{normalised} CES \citep{KlumpDeLaGrandville2000} with elasticity of
substitution~$\sigma$; firms hire endogenously, the wage follows a
Blanchflower--Oswald wage curve, investment is financed out of retained
earnings, and the model is stock-flow consistent across the parameter space.
Whether higher retention $\rho$ builds capacity or displaces workers and shrinks
the wage bill is an outcome, not an assumption.

We report three results. First, $Y(\rho)$ is \emph{U-shaped}: its turning point
$\rhostar$ rises with $\sigma$, and the retention rate anchored to the
investment rate lies \emph{left} of that turn at every elasticity tested --- so
the marginal effect there is negative, the wide-range effect positive. Read
through a single slope this presents as a sign frontier $\sigstar \approx 0.65$
--- a declared reduction, not a verdict. Second, the
region in which retention depresses output is conditional on fiscal
institutions: a balanced-budget unemployment benefit at an OECD-band
replacement rate eliminates it. Third, the wage curve is a source of fragility
immune to demand instruments --- three stabilisation hypotheses are falsified,
all through one wage$\,\to\,$unemployment$\,\to\,$capital-erosion channel.

A sixteen-parameter global sensitivity analysis then separates what generalises
from what does not. Viability is governed almost entirely by the depreciation
rate, at whose empirically implied value no sampled configuration survives.
$\sigma$ is nearly inert marginally, so the \emph{frontier} does not generalise
--- though the sign at the anchored point does, wherever the turn is resolved.
And the measured sign depends on the estimator chosen to summarise a curve.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} agent-based macroeconomics; stock-flow
consistency; elasticity of substitution; wage-led growth; global sensitivity
analysis; capital accumulation.

\end{abstract}
