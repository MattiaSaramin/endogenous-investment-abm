\begin{abstract}
\noindent
We extend the disaggregated Keynesian cross of \citet{Teglio2025} with
endogenous investment and capital accumulation, so that both sides of a
heterogeneous-agent economy are endogenous. Production is a \emph{normalised}
CES \citep{KlumpDeLaGrandville2000} with elasticity of substitution~$\sigma$;
firms hire endogenously, the wage follows a Blanchflower--Oswald wage curve,
investment is financed out of retained earnings, and the model is stock-flow
consistent across the parameter space. The Kaleckian question --- does higher
retention $\rho$ build capacity or displace workers and shrink the wage bill?
--- is an outcome rather than an assumption.

We report three results. First, the response $Y(\rho)$ is \emph{U-shaped}: its
turning point $\rhostar$ rises with $\sigma$, and the empirically anchored
retention rate lies to the \emph{left} of that turn for every
$\sigma \geq 0.5$, so the marginal effect at the anchored point is negative
while the wide-range effect is positive. Read through a single slope this
presents as a sign frontier $\sigstar \approx 0.65$, which we retain as a
declared reduction rather than a verdict. Second, the region in which retention
depresses output is conditional on fiscal institutions: a balanced-budget
unemployment benefit at an OECD-band replacement rate eliminates it entirely.
Third, the wage curve is a source of fragility immune to demand instruments ---
three stabilisation hypotheses are falsified, all through one
wage$\,\to\,$unemployment$\,\to\,$capital-erosion channel.

A global sensitivity analysis over sixteen parameters shows this conditional
structure does not survive marginalisation, that viability is governed by the
depreciation rate rather than by $\sigma$, and that the sign depends measurably
on the estimator chosen to summarise a curved response.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} agent-based macroeconomics; stock-flow
consistency; elasticity of substitution; wage-led growth; global sensitivity
analysis; capital accumulation.

\end{abstract}
