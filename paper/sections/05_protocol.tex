\section{Experimental protocol}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Steady states, not histories.}
Unless stated otherwise, every reported figure is a mean over 20 random seeds
of the last 50 observations of a 2{,}000-step run. Runs are long because the
transient must be exhausted, not because 2{,}000 periods of history are
meaningful.

\paragraph{The sign frontier estimator.}
The quantity of interest is the sign of $\mathrm{d}Y/\mathrm{d}\rho$. We
estimate an OLS slope of $Y$ on $\rho$ over the \emph{common viable support}
(the set of $\rho$ values at which every configuration being compared
survives), and define $\sigstar$ as the value of $\sigma$ at which that slope
crosses zero. Confidence intervals come from a percentile bootstrap over
2{,}000 resamples \emph{of the seeds}. Restricting to a common support matters:
comparing slopes estimated on different supports would confound the effect
under study with differential survival.

\paragraph{Nesting checks as anti-drift sentinels.}
Every extension introduces a parameter whose null value must reproduce the
previous model exactly: $\sigma = 1$ (Cobb-Douglas), $\eta = 0$ (fixed wage),
$\lambda_e = 1$ (static expectations), $rr = 0$ (no government),
$\mathit{spread} = 0$ (homogeneous firms). Each is implemented as an explicit
branch and verified \emph{artifact-against-artifact} on committed panels. The
criterion under which those checks pass or fail is itself a measured object,
and is reported in \cref{app:bytecheck}: exact bit equality turned out not to
be reproducible over time, and the replacement declares a tolerance on levels
and zero tolerance on regime.

\paragraph{The reported numbers, and what may be quoted.}
Every figure in this paper is either measured from a committed driver script or
labelled as a design target or convention. The rule is stated because it was
adopted after an audit found previously recorded ``results'' that had never
been measured, and because it has teeth: it is what required the withdrawals of
\cref{app:retractions}, and what forbids quoting an analytical identity in
place of a measured ratio when the identity's derivation no longer applies.

\paragraph{Global sensitivity design.}
For \cref{sec:sa}, $\rho$ is a \emph{treatment} rather than a swept parameter:
each design point is evaluated at several values of $\rho$ under common random
numbers (the same seeds at each, differenced or regressed per seed, then
averaged). The reported analysis uses four values of $\rho$, and they contain
the two of the superseded chord, so that both estimators are computed from the
\emph{same runs} and their difference cannot be a different sample. Without
common random numbers the quantity of interest is a difference of two noisy
numbers, and the sensitivity indices would decompose the variance of the seed
draw rather than of the model. Uniform ranges over 16 parameters are a
declared \emph{choice of ignorance}. Screening uses the elementary-effects
method of \citet{Morris1991} with a pruning rule frozen in the source before
any output existed; the survivors go to Sobol' estimation
\citep{Sobol2001,Saltelli2010} with bootstrap confidence intervals, computed
with SALib \citep{HermanUsher2017}. The number of seeds was fixed on evidence
by a pilot: the noise-to-signal ratio measured $0.207$, $0.170$ and $0.111$ at
3, 5 and 10 seeds, scaling as $1/\sqrt{n}$ as expected.

\paragraph{Implementation.}
The model is implemented in Python on the Mesa framework
\citep{Kazil2020}. The automated suite --- counted and described in
\cref{app:repro} --- covers stock-flow consistency (parametrised over the
ownership range), per-seed determinism, labour accounting, every nesting
limit, and directional regression pins. Each
committed data file has a committed generator script;
\cref{app:repro} gives the details, and \cref{app:notation} collects the
notation.
