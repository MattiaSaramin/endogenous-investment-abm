\section{Reproducibility}
\label{app:repro}

Every table in this paper is generated from committed comma-separated output
files, each of which has a committed generator script; the correspondence is
recorded in the repository. Runs pin BLAS thread counts before importing
numerical libraries, because thread-dependent reduction order would otherwise
break per-seed determinism. The execution environment is recorded alongside
the sensitivity-analysis outputs, including library versions and the frozen
sampling seed.

The suite comprises 527 automated tests. Beyond conventional unit coverage
they assert the model's economic invariants directly: stock-flow consistency
parametrised over the ownership range rather than at the default alone
(\cref{sec:ownership}), per-seed determinism, exact nesting at every limiting
parameter value, the workforce cap that restores diminishing returns
(\cref{sec:model}), and directional regression pins on the basin-selection
behaviour of initial capital.

Fifteen of those tests cover the repaired quantity of interest and the
reproducibility criterion of \cref{sec:method}. They are written to pin the
\emph{reason} for each declared constant rather than the constant itself: that
a pure ULP tolerance diverges near zero, that the regime limb must reject a
viability flip no matter how small the numerical gap, and --- as an executable
statement of the defect this paper repairs --- that a chord and a
whole-support slope can carry opposite signs on the same U-shaped response.
The last of these also records the width of the window in which they disagree,
which is narrow and computable: on the four-point support, the chord is
negative for any turning point above $0.45$ while the slope stays positive for
any turning point below $0.50$.
